# Fading Hours

A quiet 2D narrative game built in Godot Engine. You arrive in a small village to help an elderly woman named Ana piece together her lost memories — one small task at a time.

Every conversation matters. Different choices reveal different fragments of the story, and how much you uncover depends entirely on what you do and who you listen to.

---

## About the Game

**Genre:** 2D Narrative / Adventure  
**Engine:** Godot 4  
**Platform:** PC  
**Made for:** Develop at Ubisoft — Programming Track (2025/2026)

---

## Story

Ana is old, and her memories are slipping. You've come to stay with her and help around the house. But as you explore the village, you start to realize she was once someone remarkable — and the village itself holds the proof.

Bring her the right things. Talk to the right people. And maybe she'll remember.

---

## Endings

There are three endings, each shaped by your choices:

- **The Full Picture** — You find everything. Ana remembers it all.
- **Two Sides** — You stay and listen. Someone else fills in the part she can't.
- **The Quiet Ending** — You leave too soon. Some memories are lost forever.

---

## Features

- Branching dialogue system driven by a Global state manager
- Multiple NPCs with unique story fragments
- Inventory system for collecting and delivering items
- Gardening quest mechanic
- JSON-based dialogue — fully data-driven, no hardcoded story paths
- Multiple endings based on player choices

---

## How to Play

| Key | Action |
|-----|--------|
| WASD | Move |
| C | Talk to NPC |
| E | Interact / Pick up |
| Space | Stay and listen (extended dialogue) |
| Enter | Continue / Skip dialogue |

## Development

This was my first game, built solo over ~6 months with no prior game development experience.

Everything was new — the engine, GDScript, tilemaps, animation, UI. The process was: learn the basics, design the world and story, build the systems one at a time, and survive the final push.

Built with guidance from my mentor at Ubisoft as part of the **Develop at Ubisoft** mentorship program — a global initiative pairing aspiring developers with Ubisoft engineers.
